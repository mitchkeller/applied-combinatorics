You might think that something slightly more general is lurking in the background of the preceding example—and it is. Suppose we have two disjoint events \(A\) and \(B\) in a probability space \((S,P)\) and that \(P(A)+P(B)\lt 1\text{.}\) Then suppose we make repeated samples from this space with each sample independent of all previous ones. Call it a win if event \(A\) holds and a loss if event \(B\) holds. Otherwise, it’s a tie and we sample again. Now the probability of a win is:
\begin{equation*} P(A)+P(A)\sum_{n\ge 1}(1-P(A)-P(B))^n=\frac{P(A)}{P(A)+P(B)}. \end{equation*}
