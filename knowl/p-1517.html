In this figure, the numbers associated with each edge are its capacity and the amount of flow that \(\phi\) places on that edge. For example, the edge \((E,D)\) has capacity \(20\) and currently carries a flow of \(8\text{.}\) (Since \(\phi(x,y)\leq c(x,y)\text{,}\) it is always easy to determine which number is the capacity and which is the flow.) The value of this flow is \(30 = \phi(S,F)+\phi(S,B)+\phi(S,E)=\phi(A,T)+\phi(C,T)\text{.}\) To see that the second conservation law holds at, for example, vertex \(B\text{,}\) note that the flow into \(B\) is \(\phi(S,B)+\phi(E,B)+\phi(D,B) = 20\) and the flow out of \(B\) is \(\phi(B,F)+\phi(B,A)+\phi(B,C)=20\text{.}\)
