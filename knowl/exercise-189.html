Exercise 12.

A valid database identifier of length \(n\) can be constructed in three ways:
  • Starting with \(A\) and followed by any valid identifier of length \(n-1\text{.}\)
  • Starting with one of the two-character strings \(1A\text{,}\) \(1B\text{,}\) \(1C\text{,}\) \(1D\text{,}\) \(1E\text{,}\) or \(1F\) and followed by any valid identifier of length \(n-2\text{.}\)
  • Starting with \(0\) and followed by any ternary (\(\{0,1,2\}\)) string of length \(n-1\text{.}\)
Find a recurrence for the number \(g(n)\) of database identifiers of length \(n\) and then solve your recurrence to obtain an explicit formula for \(g(n)\text{.}\) (You may consider the empty string of length \(0\) a valid database identifier, making \(g(0)=1\text{.}\) This will simplify the arithmetic.)
