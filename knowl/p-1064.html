  2. We are seeking the number of solutions to the equation \(a_1 + \cdots + a_k = n\) (where \(k\) is allowed to vary) subject to \(a_i\in\{1,5,10,25\}\text{.}\) Therefore, the ordinary generating function is
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^\infty (x+x^5+x^{10}+x^{25})^k=\frac{1}{1-x-x^5-x^{10}-x^{25}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    A computer algebra system gives 1856250874024 as the coefficient on \(x^{95}\text{.}\)
