It is easy to see that
\begin{align*} \frac{d_n}{n!} \amp = \frac{\sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k\binom{n}{k}(n-k)!}{n!}\\ \amp = \sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}\frac{(n-k)!}{n!}\\ \amp = \sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k \frac{1}{k!}. \end{align*}
Recall from Calculus that the Taylor series expansion of \(e^x\) is given by
\begin{equation*} e^x = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^k}{k!}, \end{equation*}
and thus the result then follows by substituting \(x=-1\text{.}\)
