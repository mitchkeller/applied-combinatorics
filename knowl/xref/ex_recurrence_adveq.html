Example 9.8.

Suppose that the sequence \(\{s_n\colon n\geq 0\}\) satisfies \(s_0 = 3\) and \(s_{n+1} = 2s_{n}\) for \(n\geq 1\text{.}\) Find an explicit formula for \(s_n\text{.}\)

What function has the property that when we advance it, i.e., evaluate it at \(n+1\text{,}\) it gives twice the value that it takes at \(n\text{?}\) The first function that comes into your mind should be \(2^n\text{.}\) Of course, just like with our differential equation, for any constant \(c\) , \(c2^n\) also has this property. \begin{equation*} Af(n) = 3\cdot 2^{n+1} = 3\cdot 2\cdot 2^n = 2\cdot (3\cdot 2^n) = 2\cdot f(n). \end{equation*}
