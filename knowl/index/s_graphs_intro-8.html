A graph \(\GVE\) is called a complete graph when \(xy\) is an edge in \(\bfG\) for every distinct pair \(x,y\in V\text{.}\) Conversely, \(\bfG\) is an independent graph if \(xy\not\in E\text{,}\) for every distinct pair \(x,y\in V\text{.}\) It is customary to denote a complete graph on \(n\) vertices by \(\bfK_n\) and an independent graph on \(n\) vertices by \(\bfI_n\).
