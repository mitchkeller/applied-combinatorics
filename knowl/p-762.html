Let \(\PXP\) be a poset. We start our procedure by finding the following subsets of the ground set: \(\mathcal{D} = \{D(x):x\in X\}\text{.}\) We then distinguish two cases. In the first case, there are distinct elements \(x\) and \(y\) for which \(D(x)\nsubseteq D(y)\) and \(D(y)\nsubseteq D(x)\text{.}\) In this case, we choose an element \(z\in D(x)-D(y)\) and an element \(w\in D(y)-D(x)\text{.}\) It follows that the four elements in \(\{x,y,z,w\}\) form a subposet of \(\bfP\) which is isomorphic to \(\bftwo+\bftwo\text{.}\)
