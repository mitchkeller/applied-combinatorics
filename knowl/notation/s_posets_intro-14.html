Distinct points \(x\) and \(y\) in a poset \(\PXP\) are comparable if either \(x\lt y\) in \(P\) or \(x>y\) in \(P\text{;}\) otherwise \(x\) and \(y\) are incomparable. If \(x\) and \(y\) are incomparable in \(\bfP\text{,}\) we sometimes write \(x\| y\) in \(\bfP\text{.}\)With a poset \(\PXP\text{,}\) we associate a comparability graph \({\bfG}_1=(X,E_1)\) and an incomparability graph \({\bfG}_2=(X,E_2)\text{.}\) The edges in the comparability graph \({\bfG}_1\) consist of the comparable pairs and the edges in the incomparability graph are the incomparable pairs.
