Lemma 9.20.

Consider a nonhomogeneous operator equation of the form
\begin{equation} p(A)f= (c_0A^{k}+ c_1A^{k-1} + c_2A^{k-2} + \dots+c_k)f = g,\tag{9.5.2} \end{equation}
with \(c_0,c_k\neq0\text{,}\) and let \(W\) be the subspace of \(V\) consisting of all solutions to the corresponding homogeneous equation
\begin{equation} p(A)f=(c_0A^{k}+ c_1A^{k-1} + c_2A^{k-2} + \dots+c_k)f = 0.\tag{9.5.3} \end{equation}
If \(f_0\) is a solution to (9.5.2), then every solution \(f\) to (9.5.2) has the form \(f=f_0+f_1\) where \(f_1\in W\text{.}\)

Proof.
