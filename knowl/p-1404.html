Our first goal is to choose the values of \(n\text{,}\) \(s\) and \(p\) so that with high probability, a random graph does not have an independent set of size \(s\text{.}\) You might think as a second goal, we would try to get a random graph without small cycles. But this goal is too restrictive. Instead, we just try to get a graph in which there are relatively few small cycles.
