Suppose that the points of our poset \(\bfP\) are \(\{x_1,x_2,\dots,x_n\}\text{.}\) We construct a network from \(\bfP\) consisting of the source \(S\text{,}\) sink \(T\text{,}\) and two points \(x'_i\) and \(x''_i\) for each point \(x_i\) of \(\bfP\text{.}\) All edges in our network will have capacity \(1\text{.}\) We add edges from \(S\) to \(x'_i\) for \(1\leq i\leq n\) and from \(x''_i\) to \(T\) for \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\) Of course, this network wouldnâ€™t be too useful, as it has no edges from the single-prime nodes to the double-prime nodes. To resolve this, we add an edge directed from \(x'_i\) to \(x''_j\) if and only if \(x_i \lt x_j\) in \(\bfP\text{.}\)
