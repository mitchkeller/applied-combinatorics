A graph \(\bfG\) is called a bipartite graph when there is a partition of the vertex set \(V\) into two sets \(A\) and \(B\) so that the subgraphs induced by \(A\) and \(B\) are independent graphs, i.e., no edge of \(\bfG\) has both of its endpoints in \(A\) or in \(B\text{.}\) Evidently, bipartite graphs are \(2\)-colorable. On the other hand, when a \(2\)-colorable graph is disconnected, there is more than one way to define a suitable partition of the vertex set into two independent sets.
