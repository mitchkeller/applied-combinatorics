Proposition 2.9.

If \(n\) and \(k\) are integers with \(0\le k\le n\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \binom{n}{k}=C(n,k)=\frac{P(n,k)}{k!}=\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} \end{equation*}

Proof.

If \(X\) is an \(n\)-element set, then \(P(n,k)\) counts the number of \(X\)-permutations of length \(k\text{.}\) Each of the \(C(n,k)\) \(k\)-element subsets of \(X\) can be turned into \(k!\) permutations, and this accounts for each permutation exactly once. Therefore, \(k! C(n,k)=P(n,k)\) and dividing by \(k!\) gives the formula for the number of \(k\)-element subsets.
