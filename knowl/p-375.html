But at this point, Bob seemed to hit a barrier, because
\begin{equation*} f(k+1) = 2f(k) - f(k-1) = 2(2k+1) - f(k-1), \end{equation*}
using the inductive hypothesis to replace \(f(k)\) by \(2k+1\text{.}\) However, he’s was totally perplexed about what to do with the \(f(k-1)\text{.}\) If he knew that \(f(k-1)=2(k-1)+1\text{,}\) then the right hand side would result in \(2(2k+1) -(2k-1)= 2k+3=2(k+1)+1\text{,}\) which is exactly what he wants. Bob always plays by the rules, and he has to admit that he doesn’t know that \(f(k-1)=2(k-1)+1\text{.}\) He only knows that \(f(k)=2k+1\text{.}\)
