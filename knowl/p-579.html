How many trees are there with vertex set \([n]=\{1,2,\dots,n\}\text{?}\) Let \(T_n\) be this number. For \(n=1\text{,}\) there is clearly only one tree. Also, for \(n=2\text{,}\) there is only one tree, which is isomorphic to \(\bfK_2\text{.}\) In determining \(T_3\text{,}\) we finally have some work to do; however, thereâ€™s not much, since all trees on \(3\) vertices are isomorphic to \(\bfP_3\text{.}\) Thus, there are \(T_3=3\) labeled trees on \(3\) vertices, corresponding to which vertex is the one of degree \(2\text{.}\) When \(n=4\text{,}\) we can begin by counting the number of nonisomorphic trees and consider two cases depending on whether the tree has a vertex of degree \(3\text{.}\) If there is a vertex of degree \(3\text{,}\) the tree is isomorphic to \(\bfK_{1,3}\) or it does not have a vertex of degree three, in which case it is isomorphic to \(\bfP_4\text{,}\) since there must be precisely two vertices of degree \(2\) in such a graph. There are four labelings by \([4]\) for \(\bfK_{1,3}\) (choose the vertex of degree three). How many labelings by \([4]\) are there for \(\bfP_4\text{?}\) There are \(C(4,2)\) ways to choose the labels \(i,j\) given to the vertices of degree \(2\) and two ways to select one of the remaining labels to be made adjacent to \(i\text{.}\) Thus, there are \(12\) ways to label \(\bfP_4\) by \([4]\) and so \(T_4=16\text{.}\)
