Given a network, it is very easy to find a flow. We simply assign \(\phi(e)=0\) for every edge \(e\text{.}\) It is very easy to underestimate the importance of this observation, actually. Network flow problems are a special case of a more general class of optimization problems known as linear programs, and in general, it may be very difficult to find a feasible solution to a linear programming problem. In fact, conceptually, finding a feasible solution—any solution—is just as hard as finding an optimal solution.
