Let \(p\) and \(d\) be numbers with \(0\lt p\lt 1\) and \(d\ge 1\text{.}\) Also, let \(\cgF\) be a finite family of events in a probability space and for each event \(E\in\cgF\text{,}\) let \(\cgN(E)\) denote the subfamily of events from \(\cgF-\{E\}\) so that \(E\) is independent of any event not in \(\cgN(E)\text{.}\) Suppose that \(P(E)\le p\text{,}\) \(|\cgN(E)|\le d\) for every event \(E\in\cgF\) and that \(ep(d+1)\lt 1\text{,}\) where \(e=2.71828\dots\) is the base for natural logarithms. Then
\begin{equation*} P(\prod_{E\in\cgF}\overline{E})\ge\prod_{E\in\cgG}(1-x(E)), \end{equation*}
i.e., the probability that all events in \(\cgF\) is positive.
