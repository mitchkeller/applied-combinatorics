In this section, we will use generating functions to enumerate the a certain type of trees. In doing this, we will see how generating functions can be used in solving a nonlinear recurrence equation. We will also make a connection to a counting sequence we encountered back in ChapterÂ 2. To do all of this, we must introduce a bit of terminology. A tree is rooted if we have designated a special vertex called its root. We will always draw our trees with the root at the top and all other vertices below it. An unlabeled tree is one in which we do not make distinctions based upon names given to the vertices. For our purposes, a binary tree is one in which each vertex has \(0\) or \(2\) children, and an ordered tree is one in which the children of a vertex have some ordering (first, second, third, etc.). Since we will be focusing on rooted, unlabeled, binary, ordered trees (RUBOTs for short), we will call the two children of vertices that have children the left and right children.
