Let \(m\text{,}\) \(n\text{,}\) and \(c\) be positive integers. Then there exist integers \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) not necessarily non-negative, solving the linear diophantine equation \(am+bn=c\) if and only if \(c\) is a multiple of the greatest common divisor of \(m\) and \(n\text{.}\)
