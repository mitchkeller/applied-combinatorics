For posets of moderate size, diagrams are frequently used to define a poset—rather than the explicit binary relation notation illustrated in Example 6.3. In Figure 6.5, we illustrate a poset \(\PXP\) with ground set \(X=[17]=\{1,2,\dots,17\}\text{.}\) It would take several lines of text to write out the binary relation \(P\text{,}\) and somehow the diagram serves to give us a more tactile sense of the properties of the poset.
