Students in middle school are taught to add fractions by finding least common multiples. For example, the least common multiple of \(15\) and \(12\) is \(60\text{,}\) so:
\begin{equation*} \frac{2}{15}+ \frac{7}{12} = \frac{8}{60}+\frac{35}{60} = \frac{43}{60}. \end{equation*}
How hard is it to find the least common multiple of two integers?
