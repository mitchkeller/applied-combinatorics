A Sudoku puzzle is a \(9\times 9\) array of cells that when completed have the integers \(1,2,\dots,9\) appearing exactly once in each row and each column. Also (and this is what makes the puzzles so fascinating), the numbers \(1\text{,}\) \(2\text{,}\) \(3,\dots,9\) appear once in each of the nine \(3\times 3\) subsquares identified by the darkened borders.
